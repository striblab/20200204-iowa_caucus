How Amy Klobuchar performed at the Iowa Caucus

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune February 4, 2020

After a chaotic Iowa Caucus night of delayed results and uncertainty among the campaigns and public over who won, precinct-level numbers finally reveal how each candidate performed as Democratic primary voting officially kicks off.

Sen. Amy Klobuchar's campaign to convince Iowa's caucus voters of her presidential viability ended with a fourth-place finish on Monday night, just ahead of one-time frontrunner Vice President Joe Biden. Topping the stack were Vermont Sen. Bernie Sanders, South Bend, Ind. Mayor Pete Buttigieg and Sen. Elizabeth Warren of Massachuesetts, respectively.

After two rounds of balloting across Iowa, Klobuchar was allocated TK pledged national delegates to take to the Democratic National Convention in Milwaukee this July, about TK behind Sanders.

Despiting visit all 99 counties in Iowa, Klobuchar's support was strongest in the TK and TK parts of the state, which is more plainly revealed through precinct-level maps of the caucus results.
